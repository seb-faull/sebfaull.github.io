Project Title

Inktive is a tattoo discovery app that finds the right artist for you!

Being an IOS Application it connects tattoo artists to potential customers. It is used as a business/marketing tool by the artist to publish their work. As an artist, you can upload photos to your profile, as well as display where you work, how much you charge as a base minimum, the shifts you work + opening times of your parlour and the types of tattoos you have mastered. Inktive offers the ability to search for artists by location (postcode) listing artists closest to you, as well as letting you contact them directly via email. Whether you are just looking for inspiration or wanting to get a specific style done we can find the right artist for you.

  • Client: Graduate Project
  • Date: June 2017
  • Service: IOS Application

Holton Lee

Holton Lee is a wellbeing discovery centre set in 350 acres of beautiful Dorset countryside. The centre witholds a particular understanding of disabilities and mental ill-health that runs a diverse programme designed to benefit the whole person – promoting physical, mental, spiritual and social wellbeing. The app is designed as a map tour guide, showing various points on the map where visitors can stop and look for particular animals or plants - mainly parts of the estate where wheel chair access is difficult. The application also shows visitors the best routes to take and screens where they can read more about the animals. This project was developed by our small agency team of 6 called 'Hexatech' as part of a live brief for Holton Lee. I took on the junior role as a designer, copy writer and contributed to the development of the application. You can view our presentation here. The program Xcode was used to write the application in Apple's Swift programming language. Adobe CC was used for the graphics and mock ups too.

  • Client: Livability Holton Lee
  • Date: June 2016
  • Service: IOS Application

Dissertation and The New Game: Loss of Control Essay

Both the dissertation and essay about the 'loss of control' of surveillance and online privacy are my two biggest written pieces of research. The essay essentially attempts to answer the question: How does surveillance affect society? It critically analyses this and states where the line should be drawn. You can view the essay here.

The dissertation expands on the essay, drawing more themes and concepts together to investigate the extent of which the public may undervalue their personal data at a cost to privacy. The dissertation is a social science research paper, centring on the collection and analysis of email interviews from experts that work in legal and technology sectors; One of my respondents is a director at the EU Comission. Themes and concepts discussed were: Big Data, Loss of privacy, Era of the Query and Filter Bubbles. You can view the dissertation here.

  • Client: Bournemouth University
  • Date: November 2015 & January 2017
  • Service: Research

The Lad Capsule

The Lad Capsule is an IOS Geo-Location App that I designed and developed from the ground up, and have a working prototype. The Lad Capsule serves as a digital time capsule, which saves and locks away entertaining memoirs/testimonies for as long as a user wishes. Before linking the capsule to a Geo-Location they must first set a time and date for that capsule before it can be accessed and updated again. By linking a capsule to a Geo-Location it forces the users to relive moments with their friends, essentially going back in time, with the app updating itself and saving the history of the experiences. Along with the app itself I created a 20 page dossier documenting the design and development process of the app, including user personas as well (available for download here). I used Adobe Illustrator to design the bulk of the graphics for the app, and Photoshop for mockups and accompanying graphics. The app was coded in X-Code in Apple's Swift programming language. This quickly became my favourite programming language, though it was challenging at first.

  • Client: University Project
  • Date: January 2016
  • Service: IOS Application

Yumi Web App

The initial idea behind this project was to create a web app where users could view and post recipes online, and to share them on different platforms. Whilst designing the web app it was important that it's simple for the user to navigate and to understand how to use.
The application was created using PHP and MySQL to create a database - along with various other technologies for the design. The database was developed to control the data architecture of the application. Non-signed up users are able to view recipes and members that are signed up are able to log in and add recipes. All users can search/filter recipes by starters, mains, desserts and drinks. You can view the ideation, planning and development process here on my blog.

  • Client: University Project
  • Date: April 2015
  • Service: Web Development
